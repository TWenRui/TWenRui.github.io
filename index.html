How is CO2 able to trap heat?

By Tee Wen Rui & Valerie Oh Hu Xue Rong

Introduction

CO2 is able to absorb infrared radiation, which is what we feel as heat. Other gases such as O2 are unable to absorb infrared. This is why CO2 is a great greenhouse gas.

But why is CO2 able to trap infrared? It is due to the way CO2 vibrates.

CO2 modes of vibration

CO2 vibrates in a manner that causes it to have a repeated change in its dipole moments.

This frequency of vibration matches the frequency of infrared, allowing infrared to be absorbed by CO2.

The absorbed infrared is then emitted to the earth's surface, making it warmer.
